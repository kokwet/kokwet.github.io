Our Programs

Education

Kokwet CBO believes that education is key to eradication of poverty in our community. As such, It is at the core of our programs and has revolved around motivating students and parents and increasing access to primary and secondary education. We work with the government, non-profit organizations and the community in providing bursaries, awarding scholarships and fundraising for needy students, especially the girl child. We also mobilize the community in providing resources to support government efforts in establishing local schools.

Health

Good health and flourishing communities go hand in hand. We envision a community that makes informed choices regarding health. Our health programs have focused on preventive health care and wellness. Kokwet CBO educates the community members on hygiene, nutrition and raises awareness of accessible medical services. We also strive to address the problem of water and sanitation in our communities through community mobilization and advocacy, partnership with government and NGOs.

Women Empowerment

Kokwet CBO believes that empowering women is a sustainable means of eradicating poverty in our community. We recognize that women are the primary breadwinners and caretakers in many homes in our community. Empowered women would result in better social, health and economic status of families, children and the community. We partner with women groups to hold capacity building, gender advocacy and awareness programs.

Economic Empowerment

Our economic related programs revolve around agriculture and small businesses. Most families make their income from these two activities. We help community members diversify their agricultural produce and learn about the best agricultural practices to improve their yields. We connect those with small business with professionals and volunteers in the community and partners for capacity building, technical assistance and financial services.

Our Community

Our community consists of local residents, friends and partners committed to uplift the living standards of people of Kapkoros area. Kapkoros area lies under the administrative unit of Kapkoros sublocation in Trans Mara East Sub-County in Narok County, Kenya. The area is about 60km from the famous Maasai Mara game reserve.

Our community gets its name 'kokwet' from the local language. In a traditional setting, the 'kokwet' was the a significant economic and social unit within the community. The term is now colloquially used to mean 'neighbourhood'. Here is some quick information about our people, area and partners.

People

Kapkoros area has about 7500 people and about 17 square km in size. The residents derive their income from farming and small businesses. The main cash crop is maize, tomatoes, sweat potatoes and beans even though it is in small-scale. Income from small business like sale of milk, vegetables, chicken, and livestock, charcoal trade and wage labour supplement income from cash crops.

In many households, Women are the main bread winners.

The social setting is built on trust and solidarity. Each person is encouraged to be an active player in society and to support those in need.

Area

With rural setting and distant from major towns, Kapkoros area has limited infrastructure. There is one gravelled road, three public and two private primary schools, two new secondary schools, one dispensary and two dams. Government projects yet to be completed include a bore hole and a cattle dip. There are also plans underway to supply electricity. Currently, residents fetch water from dams which is rarely treated.

The area is a lowland with black cotton soil and has a few rocky mountains with red volcanic soil and natural forests. These make the area good for agriculture however, poor agricultural practices and destructions of forests is quickly degrading the environment

Partners

We have primarily worked with local schools, government units, religious organizations, agricultural workers and self-help groups.

Our Challenges

Education Access
To help parents and children embrace learning, to increase access to primary and secondary education and to reduce social, health and economic problems that detract students from learning.
Health
To provide holistic health and wellness education and increase access to basic health care and good nutrition.
Water and Sanitation
To carry out low-cost water catchment and treatment activities and develop low-cost sanitation facilities.
Food Security
To help the community embrace sustainable agricultural practices and technology in order to increase yield.
Women Empowerment
To advocate for gender equality in households and support girl child education through fighting FGM and Women rights abuses.
Environmental Degradation
To provide environmental education, raise public awareness and work with community in pursuit of sustainable income generating activities.
