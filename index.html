Our Programs

Education

Kokwet CBO believes in education as the key to eradication of poverty. Our education programs have revolved around motivating students and increasing access to primary and secondary schools within our community. These programs have been established to curb school dropouts, unmotivated students and parents and poverty within the community that prevents many students from affording primary and secondary education. We work with the government, non-profit organizations and the community in providing bursaries, awarding scholarships and fundraising for needy students, especially the girl child. We have also mobilized the community in providing resources to support government efforts in establishing local schools.

Health

Good health and flourishing communities go hand in hand. We envision a community that makes informed health choices. As such our health programs have focused on preventive health care and wellness. Kokwet CBO educates the community members on hygiene, nutrition and raises awareness on access to medical care. We focus on school children as they are most vulnerable and women as they are the primary caretakers in most homes. We also strive to address the problem of water and sanitation in our communities through community mobilization and advocacy and partnership with government and NGOs.

Women Empowerment

Kokwet CBO believes that empowering women is a sustainable means of eradicating poverty in our community. We recognize that women are the primary breadwinners and caretakers in many homes in our community. The well being of families, children and homes is therefore dependent on them. Empowered women would result in better children healthcare, better-educated children, well-nourished children and happy homes in general. We partner with women groups to hold programs to educate parents and help build their capacity to better take care of their homes.

Ecomomic Empowerment

Our economic related programs revolve around agriculture and small businesses. Most families make their income from these two activities. Our goal is to help community members diversify their agricultural produce and learn about the best agricultural practices to improve their yields. We also hope to help community members access financial services for the benefit of their business. Ultimately, we hope that an increase in the income of community members will promote education, health and their overall well-being and reduced conflicts.

Our Community

Our community consists of local residents, friends and partners committed to uplift the living standards of people of Kapkoros area. Kapkoros area lies under the administrative unit of Kapkoros sublocation in Trans Mara East Sub-County in Narok County, Kenya. The area is about 60km from the famous Maasai Mara game reserve.

Our community gets its name 'kokwet' from the local language. In a traditional setting, the 'kokwet' was the a significant economic and social unit within the community. The term is now colloquially used to mean 'neighbourhood'. Here is some quick information about our people, area and partners.

People

Kapkoros area has about 7500 people and about 17 square km in size. The residents derive their income from farming and small businesses. The main cash crop is maize, tomatoes, sweat potatoes and beans even though it is in small-scale. Income from small business like sale of milk, vegetables, chicken, and livestock, charcoal trade and wage labour supplement income from cash crops.

In many households, Women are the main bread winners.

The social setting is built on trust and solidarity. Each person is encouraged to be an active player in society and to support those in need.

Area

With rural setting and distant from major towns, Kapkoros area has limited infrastructure. There is one gravelled road, three public and two private primary schools, two new secondary schools, one dispensary and two dams. Government projects yet to be completed include a bore hole and a cattle dip. There are also plans underway to supply electricity. Currently, residents fetch water from dams which is rarely treated.

The area is a lowland with black cotton soil and has a few rocky mountains with red volcanic soil and natural forests. These make the area good for agriculture however, poor agricultural practices and destructions of forests is quickly degrading the environment

Partners

We have partnered with local schools, government units, agricultural workers and self-help groups in education and agricultural programs.

We have partnered with Hooper Trust in supporting needy local students access secondary education, especially the girl child.

Our Core Values

Education –Reducing poverty through education by improved performance and assisting bright students with bursaries so as to complete secondary education.
Health-ensuring holistic care of mental, physical, social and spiritual being through health education and community participation and the practice of basic health practices
Agriculture-through partnership we assist the community embrace new technology to increase production and diversity.
Environment-assist in environmental practices for sustainability.
Conflict resolution-We encourage peaceful coexistence for prosperity
Gender equity-all people are equal and can work together for achieving Development.
Partnership and Networking-We encourage working with Faith based Organizations and other like minded people to realize our objective.
Transparency and accountability- we always seek to be open in all our dealings and undertakings

Our Leaders

Francis Koskei

Director

“I have been involved in community work in our area - Kapkoros, for the last eighteen years. Food security, clean water, poor sanitation, environmental degradation and tribal conflicts have been our biggest challenges. My vision for Kokwet CBO is to eradicate these challenges through education, community-participation and partnerships. I invite you to get involved.”

Francis is a clinical officer and working as part-time student Health Officer with the Kilgoris project.

Daniel Kirui

Programs Officer

"My vision of Kokwet CBO is to alleviate poverty in Kapkoros sub-location and it’s neighborhoods through education in: -Agroforestry and Apiculture, Poultry Keeping, Tree planting in schools, empowerment of the community through capacity building and conflict resolution.Partner with us to improve the living standards of our community."

Daniel is a social worker working with the Catholic Justice and Peace Commission as a volunteer.

Caroline Baraza

Finance Officer

"I have seen Kokwet CBO help improve the performance of students in our local primary schools. In 2014, Kokwet CBO was able to bring together students from six local schools for academic competitions and motivational talks.These students performed better in the national examinations compared to the students in the previous years. About 10 of the students joined the best schools in the country. We welcome you to join us in promoting healthy competitions in our primary schools."

Caroline is a patient attendant at a private clinic.

Philip Rotich

Operations Officer

“Our community - Kapkoros sub-location, is one of the poorest in our county and the schools within the sub-location have been performing poorly. This is why we started Kokwet CBO. Holding field days in schools has been one of our successful programs. In 2014, we had five field in schools. We held motivational talks and covered health education namely sanitation and hygiene, nutrition, de-worming of students, H.I.V/Aids awareness and discouraged FGM, early pregnancies and early marriages. Through such programs, we will continue to work to eradicate poverty in our community.”

Philip is a clinical officer in the Masai Mara.
