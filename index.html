Our Community

Kapkoros Sublocation

Community members connecting community members to opportunites for development

Francis Koskei

Director

“I have been involved in community work in our area - Kapkoros, for the last eighteen years. Food security, clean water, poor sanitation, environmental degradation and tribal conflicts have been our biggest challenges. My vision for Kokwet CBO is to eradicate these challenges through education, community-participation and partnerships. I invite you to get involved.”

Francis is a clinical officer running a private clinic and working as part time student Health Officer with the Kilgoris project.

Daniel Kirui

Programs Officer

"My vision of Kokwet CBO is to alleviate poverty in Kapkoros sub-location and it’s neighborhoods through education in: -Agroforestry and Apiculture, Poultry Keeping, Tree planting in schools, empowerment of the community through capacity building and conflict resolution.Partner with us to improve the living standards of our community."

Daniel is a social worker working with the Catholic Justice and Peace Commission as a volunteer.

Philip Rotich

Operations Officer

“Our community - Kapkoros sub-location, is one of the poorest in our county and the schools within the sub-location have been performing poorly. This is why we started Kokwet CBO. Holding field days in schools has been one of our successful programs. So far we have had 3 field days in 2014. In these we have been able to hold motivational talks and cover health education namely sanitation and hygiene, de-worming of students, H.I.V/Aids awareness and discouraged FGM, early pregnancies and early marriages. Through such programs, we will continue to work to eradicate poverty in our community.”

Philip is a clinical officer in the Masai Mara.

Caroline Baraza

Finance Officer

"I have seen Kokwet CBO help improve the performanceof students in our local primary schools. In 2014, Kokwet CBO was able to bring together students from six local schools for academic competitions and motivational talks.These students performed better in the national examinations compared tothe students in 2013. About 10 of the students joined the best schools in the country. We welcome you to join us in promoting healthy competions in our primary schools."

Caroline is a patient attendant at a private clinic.

“Once you educate the boys, they tend to leave the villages and go search for work in the cities, but the girls stay home, become leaders in the community, and pass on what they’ve learned. If you really want to change a culture, to empower women, improve basic hygiene and health care, and fight high rates of infant mortality, the answer is to educate girls.”
